This Cookie Policy explains how Vedarc Legal uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on the Platform, and how you can control your preferences. It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.
1. What Are Cookies
Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognise your device, retain your preferences, and understand how you interact with the site. We also use similar technologies such as pixel tags and web beacons, referred to collectively as "cookies" in this Policy.
2. Cookies We Use
2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies
These cookies are essential for the Platform to function - maintaining your login session and enabling secure navigation. They are activated automatically and do not require your consent. You cannot opt out of these cookies without also opting out of the Platform entirely.
2.2 Analytics and Performance Cookies
We use analytics tools - such as Google Analytics - to understand how users interact with the Platform: which pages are most visited, where difficulties arise, and how sessions progress. All data is aggregated and anonymised. These cookies are activated only with your consent.
2.3 Marketing and Targeting Cookies
Where we run promotional campaigns, we may use third-party marketing tools - such as Meta Pixel - to serve relevant advertisements to users who have previously visited the Platform. These cookies are activated only with your explicit consent and are not used to track activity unrelated to our services.
3. Your Cookie Preferences
On your first visit to the Platform, you will be presented with a cookie consent notice. You may accept or decline non-essential cookies by category. You may review and update your preferences at any time via the cookie settings link in the Platform footer.
You may also manage cookies through your browser settings. Blocking all cookies may affect Platform functionality, including your ability to remain logged in.
4. Cookie Retention
Session cookies expire when you close your browser. Persistent analytics and marketing cookies - where consented - are retained for up to six (6) months, unless cleared earlier through your browser or preference settings.
5. Third-Party Cookies
Some cookies are set by third-party services we use. These third parties operate under their own privacy policies. We do not control third-party cookies and are not responsible for third-party data practices. We disclose all third-party cookies in use on the Platform via the cookie consent management tool.
